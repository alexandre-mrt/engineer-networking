# Research: Networking Strategies for Engineers

## Foundational Frameworks

### Keith Ferrazzi "Never Eat Alone"
- Lead with generosity: offer before asking
- Build relationships before you need them
- Authenticity and vulnerability deepen connections
- "Ping" constantly to stay connected

### Adam Grant "Give and Take"
- Three styles: Givers, Takers, Matchers
- Five-Minute Favor: help anyone for 5 min or less
- Brokering introductions drives innovation
- Givers achieve top success long-term

### Reid Hoffman "The Alliance"
- Tours of Duty: Rotational (learn), Transformational (grow), Foundational (commit)
- Network intelligence as strategic value
- Alumni networks as career-long assets

## Hidden Job Market Data
- 70-85% of jobs never posted online
- Referrals 15x more likely to be hired vs job boards
- Referrals = 7% of applicants but 30-50% of hires
- 46% of referral hires stay past 1 year vs 14% job board hires

## Weak & Dormant Ties Theory
- Mark Granovetter (1973, validated 2023): weak ties connect to outside networks
- Weak ties matter MOST in software (LinkedIn study, MIT 2022)
- Dormant ties provide both novelty (weak) and trust (strong) benefits
- 3 keys to reconnection: remembering, catching up, shared perception

## Introvert Strategies
- 60-65% of engineers are introverts
- Quality > quantity: build 5-8 deep relationships
- Schedule 30-45 min networking + recovery time at events
- Content as passive networking engine (blog, open source)

## LinkedIn Optimization
- Optimized profiles get 20-40x more views
- Headline: 100+ chars with keywords carries algorithmic weight
- Professional photo: 21x more views, 9x more connection requests
- Verified skills boost hiring 30%
- Recruiters spend 6 seconds per profile — front-load achievements

## Personal Branding
- Pick ONE channel: blogging, speaking, or open source
- 1 technical post/month establishes expertise
- 1-2 talks/year attracts recruiters and mentors
- "From 2025, it's not what you code, but who knows you can code"

## Cold Outreach
- Cold email: 1-5% reply rate. LinkedIn InMail: 18-25%
- 50-125 words = highest reply rate (50% higher than longer)
- Personalization triples response rate
- Research-driven messages only ones that get noticed

## Network Maintenance
- Dunbar's Number: ~150 meaningful relationships
- 5-15-50-150 Model: weekly / monthly / quarterly / annual touchpoints
- Personal CRMs (Dex, Clay, Monica) exceed Dunbar limits
- Monthly newsletter keeps 500+ contacts engaged

## Key Statistics
- 85% of jobs landed through networking
- Referral hires cost $1K vs $4-18K traditional recruitment
- Open source connects you globally + teaches soft skills
- Volunteering at events = 20-30 high-quality contacts
